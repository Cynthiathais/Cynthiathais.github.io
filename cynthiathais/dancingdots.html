Dancing Dots

B IMD 362 WI 19'

Description
An iOS app that utilizes NSArrays in Objective-C to creatively demonstrate visual design fundamentals.

Role
UI/UX + Visual Design

    • Main Objective

      As described by authors Ellie Lupton and Jennifer Cole Phillips in their book Graphic Design the New Basics, balance and rhythm are fundamental elements of graphic design stating: "Balance and rhythm work together to create works of design that pulse with life, achieving both stability and surprise". Point, line, and plane are the building blocks of design, which I utilized to demonstrate my understanding of the concepts of rhythm and balance through the use of NSArrays in Objective-C for an iOS app.

    • Design Process

      Dancing Dots is an app that plays with line and points to create a sense of rhythm and balance as the user clicks through the screens. The dots are made up of quadrants, each having a different color. The arangment of the dots change as the users clicks on the segmented control, giving the illusion that the dots are moving. I chose a fun color palette, contrasting the bright colors with different shades of grey. The quadrants in each circle create lines when put next to other circles in my composition. The alternating pattern of colors create lines diagonally, giving the design a sense of rhythm as the dots change position. I chose a font that was playful and had round corners to match the roundness of the dots, pairing it with a thin font for the words "rhythm and balance" to create a contrast. At the top of my screen, I added an NSArray object to hold "text", which I used to add to the movement and overall balance of my composition; I chose alternating colors that matched my dots.

    Reflection

    The technical requirements for this app were challenging for me as I was in the early process of learning this new coding language. This app allowed me to better understand and think about using NSArrays creatively, demonstrating both my technical skills, as well as my design skills. I challenged myself to use the IOCTextInput Class creatively to make my app visually stronger. The alternating, symetrical patterns created by the text reitterates the ideas presented by Lupton and Phillips.
